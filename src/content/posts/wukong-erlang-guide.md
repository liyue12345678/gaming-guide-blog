Erlang, the Sacred Divinity, is the secret true final boss of Black Myth: Wukong. He's harder than the actual final boss — and beating him unlocks the **true ending**. This fight demands a specific strategy; you can't just out-stat him.

## How to Access Erlang

You must complete all secret areas across all chapters:

1. Chapter 1: Ring 3 bells → defeat Elder Jinchi
2. Chapter 2: Complete Fuban quest → defeat Fuban
3. Chapter 3: Find and complete Green-Capped Martialist quest
4. Chapter 4: Defeat the Duskveil in Purple Cloud Mountain
5. Chapter 5: Defeat Bishui Golden-Eyed Beast in Bishui Cave

After completing all 5 secret areas, return to the **Great Pagoda** in Chapter 3. A new path opens to Erlang's arena.

## Why Erlang Is So Hard

- **4 phases**, each with a recharging shield bar
- Shield blocks nearly all damage — you must break it first
- Ultra-aggressive AI with mix-ups and delayed attacks
- Can punish healing and spell-casting
- Phase 3 adds a giant form and aerial attacks

The Plantain Fan is the only thing that breaks his shield reliably.

## Phase-by-Phase Strategy

### Phase 1 (100% HP — Full Shield)

Erlang starts with his shield active and a standard glaive moveset.

**His attacks**:
- Glaive combo (3-5 hits with delays)
- Thrust into sweep
- Aerial slam with AoE
- Grapple that throws you

**Strategy**:

1. **Open with Plantain Fan** — breaks his entire shield bar instantly
2. Rush in, **Immobilize**, unleash full light combo + Wandering Wight spirit
3. He'll recover and reshield after about 15 seconds
4. Play defensive until your Qi refills
5. Use **Azure Dust transformation** if he corners you — he can't counter it
6. Repeat: Fan → Immobilize → damage → wait → Fan

Phase 1 is the easiest. If you're dying here, you're not using the Fan enough.

### Phase 2 (75% HP — New Attacks)

Erlang gains new lightning-empowered attacks and becomes more aggressive.

**New attacks**:
- Lightning glaive slam (AoE cone)
- Rapid thrust combo with tracking
- Thunderbolt calls from the sky

**Strategy**:

- Same Fan → Immobilize loop
- Cloud Step when you see lightning gathering — the thunderbolt call tracks
- His combos are now 5-7 hits. Don't try to parry all of them. Dodge the first 3, Rock Solid on hit 4 or 5.
- Use Azure Dust whenever you need breathing room

### Phase 3 (50% HP — Giant Form)

Erlang transforms into a giant celestial form. His attacks are slower but cover the entire arena.

**New attacks**:
- Giant glaive sweep (covers 180°)
- Foot stomp with shockwave
- Laser beam from third eye
- Summons Four Heavenly Kings' weapons

**Strategy**:

- The Fan **still works** on his giant form shield
- Dodge into his stomp, not away — you have more i-frames
- The laser beam is easy to dodge but one-shots if it hits. Just sprint perpendicular.
- This phase is more intimidating than hard. Stay calm, wait for Fan charges, punish after shield breaks.

### Phase 4 (25% HP — Desperation)

Erlang returns to normal size but gains his full moveset from all phases.

**Strategy for the final push**:

- At this point you should have 1-2 Fan charges ready
- Pop ALL buffs — this is the last push
- Fan → Immobilize → Azure Dust spam → Wandering Wight → everything
- If he's below 10%, you can trade hits with your remaining heals
- Cloud Step the grab — it's his fastest attack and kills if you're below 70% HP

## After Erlang: Four Heavenly Kings

Beat Erlang and you enter a cinematic gauntlet against the Four Heavenly Kings. This fight is a spectacle, not a challenge — simplified movesets, giant form, mostly a victory lap.

## Common Mistakes

<div class="warning-box">
<strong>⚠️ Don't Do These:</strong>
<ul>
<li><strong>Using the Fan when his shield is already down</strong> — save it for when the blue bar reappears</li>
<li><strong>Standing still to heal</strong> — Erlang input-reads healing. Only heal during Cloud Step or after a shield break.</li>
<li><strong>Using Pillar Stance</strong> — his AI hard-counters it with instant sweeps</li>
<li><strong>Trying to out-stat him</strong> — you can't. This is a mechanics fight. Master the Fan loop.</li>
<li><strong>Saving Azure Dust for "emergencies"</strong> — use it on cooldown. It charges faster than you think.</li>
</ul>
</div>
