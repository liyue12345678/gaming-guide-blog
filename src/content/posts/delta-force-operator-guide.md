Your operator choice in Delta Force defines your entire playstyle. Unlike games where operators are just "different flavors of gunplay," Delta Force operators have distinct gadgets that fundamentally change how you approach combat.

## S-Tier Operators

### Stinger (Support) — The Best Operator in the Game

**Why S-tier**: The healing pistol revives teammates at range and heals faster than any medkit. A good Stinger makes the entire squad nearly unkillable.

**Gadget**: Adrenaline Injector — heal or revive at range. 3 charges, fast recharge.

**How to play Stinger**:
- Stay behind your squad, not in front
- Use cover aggressively — you heal while peeking
- Save at least 1 charge for a clutch revive
- Pair with an Engineer — you heal them, they suppress the enemy
- **Do not** frontline. A dead Stinger helps no one.

**Best weapons**: SMG-45 (mid-range SMG), MP5 (close range), K416 (AR if you prefer range)

### Luna (Recon) — Wallhacks Win Games

**Why S-tier**: Recon Arrow reveals enemies through walls in a large radius. Information wins fights before they start.

**Gadget**: Recon Arrow — fires an arrow that pulses enemy positions. 2 charges.

**How to play Luna**:
- Fire Recon Arrow before pushing any objective
- Ping revealed enemies for your team
- Hold mid-range angles with a marksman rifle — your team needs you alive for info
- Arrow common flank routes, not the main entrance
- Communicate arrow timing — "arrow up, 2 enemies behind the truck"

**Best weapons**: AWM (long-range sniper), VSS (aggressive marksman), R93 (mobile sniper)

## A-Tier Operators

### D-Wolf (Assault) — The Aggressive Entry

**Why A-tier**: Slide mechanic and frag grenades make D-Wolf the best operator for aggressive pushes and clearing close angles.

**Gadget**: Frag Grenades — 3 impact grenades with short fuse.

**How to play D-Wolf**:
- Slide into rooms, not walk — the speed boost throws off enemy aim
- Frag corners where enemies commonly hold
- Push after your Recon gives intel
- Don't slide into unknown spaces without info
- Combo with Stinger healing for aggressive holds

**Best weapons**: CI-19 (slow but hard-hitting), M4A1 (all-rounder), SG552 (aggressive)

### Shepherd (Engineer) — Vehicle Killer & Area Denial

**Why A-tier**: The AT4 rocket launcher deletes vehicles and the deployable cover provides instant protection in open areas.

**Gadget**: AT4 Launcher + Deployable Cover — anti-vehicle explosive and portable wall.

**How to play Shepherd**:
- On vehicle maps, stay near open lanes — vehicles WILL come
- Deploy cover for your Recon/Sniper — they'll love you
- AT4 also works on clustered infantry — don't save it only for vehicles
- Pair with Stinger — you suppress, they heal
- The deployable cover blocks LMG fire — use it to create crossfires

**Best weapons**: PKM (LMG, high capacity), M7 Battle Rifle (meta), SCAR-H (heavy hitter)

## Class Synergy — The Winning Formula

A balanced squad should have:

```
1 Support (Stinger)  → Keeps everyone alive
1 Recon (Luna)       → Intel before every push
1 Assault (D-Wolf)   → Entry fragger
1 Engineer (Shepherd) → Anti-vehicle + cover
```

If your squad is missing a role, you're playing at a disadvantage. Fill what's missing.

## Common Mistakes by Operator

| Operator | Mistake | Fix |
|----------|---------|-----|
| Stinger | Playing frontline, dying first | Stay behind, heal through peeks |
| Luna | Not using arrows, holding W | Arrow before every push, check minimap |
| D-Wolf | Sliding into 3 enemies alone | Wait for intel, coordinate push |
| Shepherd | Saving AT4 for "the perfect moment" | If you see a vehicle, shoot it |
| Toxik | Placing poison in obvious spots | Trap flank routes, not main entrances |
| Hackclaw | Flanking without communicating | Tell your team before you go for a flank |
| Uluru | Deploying turret in the open | Turrets need cover to survive more than 5 seconds |

## Quick Decision Guide

**Pick Stinger if**: Your squad has no support. (Always a good pick.)

**Pick Luna if**: You're on a large map with long sightlines. Information is power.

**Pick D-Wolf if**: You're the aggressive player and your squad has a support.

**Pick Shepherd if**: You hear enemy vehicles or you're on a vehicle-heavy map.
