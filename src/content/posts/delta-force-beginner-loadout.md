Delta Force is a hardcore tactical shooter with two distinct game modes and dozens of weapons. Picking the wrong gun or attachment as a new player can make the game feel impossible. This guide sets you up for success from match one.

## Which Operator Should You Pick First?

Delta Force has four classes. For beginners, pick based on your playstyle:

| Class | Best Beginner Pick | Why |
|-------|-------------------|-----|
| **Assault** | D-Wolf | Straightforward AR gameplay, no complex gadgets |
| **Support** | Stinger | Medkit gadget keeps you alive longer while learning |
| **Engineer** | Shepherd | LMG playstyle is forgiving — spray and suppress |
| **Recon** | Luna | Recon arrow reveals enemies, helping you with map awareness |

<div class="tip-box">
<strong>💡 Recommendation:</strong> Start with <strong>Support (Stinger)</strong>. The healing gadget means you survive mistakes that would kill other classes. You can heal yourself and teammates — contributing even when your aim isn't great yet.
</div>

## Best Beginner Weapons

Don't chase the meta on day one. These weapons have the most forgiving recoil and let you focus on learning positioning.

### M4A1 (Assault Class) — Best All-Rounder

The M4A1 is the most consistent beginner weapon in the game. It handles well at all ranges and has the most manageable recoil pattern.

**Beginner Loadout**:
- **Muzzle**: Sandstorm Vertical Compensator (reduces vertical kick)
- **Barrel**: AR Gabriel Long Barrel (extends range without sacrificing handling)
- **Foregrip**: Secret Order Bevel Foregrip (improves overall control)
- **Mag**: 45-Round Extended Mag (fewer reloads = fewer vulnerable moments)
- **Stock**: M16A4 Stable Stock (reduces sway while ADS)
- **Optic**: Panoramic Red Dot (clean sight picture, fast target acquisition)
- **Sidearm**: 93R (3-round burst pistol — the most reliable backup weapon)

### MP5 (Support Class) — Close Quarters King

The MP5 dominates indoor fights and objectives. Low recoil, fast fire rate, and highly mobile.

**Beginner Loadout**:
- **Muzzle**: Titanium Muzzle Brake (controls the minor horizontal drift)
- **Foregrip**: RK-0 Foregrip
- **Mag**: 40-Round Extended Magazine
- **Stock**: Collapsed Stock (more mobility when ADSing)
- **Optic**: Holo Sight (wider view than red dots for CQB)
- **Sidearm**: G18 (full-auto pistol for "oh no" moments)

### CAR-15 (Assault Class) — Ultimate Beginner Rifle

The CAR-15 is purpose-built for new players. Lowest recoil in its class, fast handling, and very forgiving if you miss shots.

**Beginner Loadout**:
- **Muzzle**: Vertical Compensator
- **Barrel**: Standard Long Barrel
- **Foregrip**: Angled Foregrip (reduces first-shot kick)
- **Mag**: Extended Quickdraw Mag (faster reloads)
- **Optic**: Red Dot Sight

## Attachment Priority — What to Unlock First

When spending weapon XP on attachments, prioritize in this order:

1. **Recoil Control** — grips and compensators first. A gun you can control = a gun that kills.
2. **Extended Mags** — 10-15 extra bullets means you survive 2v1 encounters.
3. **Optics** — a clear sight picture improves accuracy more than any stat.
4. **Barrel** — range and velocity upgrades after you can control the base weapon.
5. **Stock** — fine-tuning weapon feel, unlock last.

<div class="warning-box">
<strong>⚠️ Don't Stack Identical Stats:</strong> Attachments that boost the same stat suffer from diminishing returns. If your muzzle already reduces vertical recoil, don't use a grip that does the same — pick one that addresses horizontal recoil instead.
</div>

## Game Mode Survival Tips

### Havoc Warfare (Large-Scale Battles)
- **Stick to cover** — running in the open is a death sentence. Move from cover to cover.
- **Watch the minimap** — friendly recon reveals enemies. Check it constantly.
- **Spawn on Squad** — always spawn on teammates, not default spawn points. You'll be in the action faster.
- **Vehicles are not tanks** — an LMG will shred a vehicle in seconds. Don't get cocky.

### Hazard Operations (Extraction Mode)
- **Survival > Loot** — extracting with basic gear is better than dying with high-tier loot.
- **Use suppressors** — they keep you off the mini-map and make third-partying harder.
- **Med crates win runs** — always carry at least 2 medical items before heading to an objective.
- **Listen for footsteps** — audio cues are more important than visual ones in close quarters.

## Three Habits That Will 10x Your Performance

### 1. Pre-Aim Every Corner
Don't round corners with your crosshair at the ground. Keep your crosshair at head height and pre-aim common enemy positions before you see them.

### 2. Reload Behind Cover, Never in the Open
The reload animation leaves you completely vulnerable. If you win a fight and need to reload, move to cover first — the next enemy is already rushing your position.

### 3. Ping Everything
Use the ping system aggressively. Ping enemies, ping loot, ping where you're moving. Communication wins games at every skill level, and pings don't require a microphone.

## The First 10 Matches — A Roadmap

1. **Matches 1-3**: Pick Support with Stinger. Stick to your squad. Focus on healing teammates and watching how they move.
2. **Matches 4-6**: Switch to Assault with the M4A1 loadout above. Start engaging enemies from medium range.
3. **Matches 7-10**: Try the MP5 on indoor sections of maps. Learn the difference between long-range and CQB playstyles.
4. **After match 10**: You'll know which class feels right. Now invest all weapon XP into that class's primary weapon.
