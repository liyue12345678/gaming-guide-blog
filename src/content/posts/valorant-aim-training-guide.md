Good aim isn't about talent — it's about deliberate practice. This guide covers the exact routines, settings, and drills that will improve your Valorant mechanics faster than grinding ranked.

## The 3 Pillars of Valorant Aim

| Pillar | What It Means | Weight |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| **Crosshair Placement** | Where your crosshair sits when you're not shooting | **60%** of aim |
| **Movement + Shooting** | Stopping accurately before firing (deadzoning) | **25%** of aim |
| **Flicking & Tracking** | Raw mouse control | **15%** of aim |

Most players practice flicking because it's fun. The players who rank up practice crosshair placement because it's what actually wins duels.

## Crosshair Settings — Keep It Simple

A good crosshair is easy to see, doesn't block vision, and doesn't distract.

### Pro-Recommended Settings

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Color** | Cyan (#00FFFF) or Green (#00FF00) — highest contrast on most maps |
| **Outlines** | On, 1px, 50% opacity |
| **Center Dot** | Off (or on at 1px if you prefer) |
| **Inner Lines** | 1-4-2-2 (thickness-length-gap-opacity) |
| **Outer Lines** | Off — reduces visual clutter |
| **Fade** | Off — keep crosshair visible while spraying |
| **Movement Error** | Off — learn deadzoning timing yourself |
| **Firing Error** | On — shows you what the spray is doing |

### Crosshair Codes to Import

```
TenZ-style:  0;s;1;P;c;5;h;0;f;0;0l;4;0o;2;0a;1;0f;0;1b;0
Simple dot:  0;s;1;P;c;5;o;1;d;1;z;3;f;0;0b;0;1b;0
Classic:     0;s;1;P;c;5;h;0;0l;4;0o;2;0a;1;0f;0;1t;0;1l;1;1o;1;1a;1;1m;0;1e;0
```

## The 10-Minute Warmup (Pre-Ranked)

Do this before every ranked session. No exceptions.

| Time | Drill | What to Do |
|------|-------|------------|
| 0:00–2:00 | **Range — Easy Bots** | Shoot 100 bots. Focus on accuracy, not speed. 90%+ headshot rate. |
| 2:00–4:00 | **Range — Medium Bots** | Push speed. Aim for 25+ kills. |
| 4:00–6:00 | **Range — Strafe Practice** | Turn on strafe bots. Practice counter-strafing while shooting. |
| 6:00–8:00 | **Deathmatch 1** | Guardian only. Forces headshots. Don't care about KD — care about clean kills. |
| 8:00–10:00 | **Deathmatch 2** | Vandal/Phantom. Play normally, but focus on crosshair placement while moving. |

<div class="tip-box">
<strong>💡 Key:</strong> The warmup is about <strong>quality, not quantity</strong>. If you're autopiloting through Deathmatch, you're wasting time. Stay mentally engaged — every shot should be deliberate.
</div>

## Crosshair Placement — The 60% Skill

Crosshair placement means keeping your crosshair at head height on the angle where an enemy will appear — before they appear.

### How to Practice

1. **In custom games, walk every map** — Trace common angles with your crosshair at head height
2. **Use map geometry as head-height reference** — Most boxes and ledges are at head height
3. **Pre-aim every corner** — Don't look at the ground while rotating
4. **Record your games** — Watch for moments your crosshair drifts to body/leg level

### Common Mistake: Looking at the Wall

When hugging a wall while rotating, look at where an enemy *would be* if they peeked, not at the wall. Your crosshair should float 1-2 character widths away from the corner, at head height.

## Movement — The 25% Skill

Valorant punishes movement while shooting harder than any other tactical FPS. Spraying while moving = bullets go to Narnia.

### Deadzoning (Counter-Strafing)

When you release a movement key, there's a brief window where your velocity hits zero and you're perfectly accurate. The timing is:
- Press A → Release A → **Shoot** → Press D

Practice this rhythm until it's muscle memory. You should be able to fire 2-3 bullet bursts between strafes with perfect accuracy.

### When to Walk vs. Run

| Situation | Movement |
|-----------|----------|
| Rotating through safe territory | Run (knife out if truly safe) |
| Approaching an angle | Walk (hold Shift) in the last 5m |
| Holding an angle | Stand still |
| Peeking an angle | Strafe out (A or D), never W |
| After missing a shot | Strafe immediately — don't stand still spraying |

## Flicking & Tracking — The 15% Skill

This is what most players think "aim training" is. It matters, but less than the other two.

### Efficient Flick Practice (5 min/day)

Use Aim Lab or Kovaak's. The only two scenarios that reliably translate to Valorant:

1. **Sixshot** (Aim Lab) — Small, static dots. Teaches precise micro-flicks.
2. **Gridshot** (Aim Lab) — Large-area flicks. Builds speed and mouse control.

Don't spend more than 10 minutes on aim trainers. They're supplementary — actual Valorant gameplay builds game-specific aim better.

## Sensitivity — One Setting to Rule Them All

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| **eDPI** (DPI × in-game sens) | 200–400 for most players |
| **DPI** | 800 (standard), 400 or 1600 also common |
| **In-game sens** | Adjust to hit 200–400 eDPI range |
| **Scoped sens** | 1.0 (matches unscoped feel) |

### How to Find Your Perfect Sens

1. Go to the Range
2. Pick a wall with a mark
3. Strafe back and forth while keeping your crosshair on the mark
4. If your crosshair lags behind → increase sens slightly
5. If your crosshair overshoots → decrease sens slightly
6. Once locked, **never change it again** — consistency beats optimization

<div class="warning-box">
<strong>⚠️ Sens-Changing Trap:</strong> Changing sensitivity every day because you had one bad match is the #1 way to never improve. Pick one sens. Stick with it for a month. Your brain needs consistency to build muscle memory.
</div>

## 30-Day Improvement Plan

| Week | Focus | Daily Routine |
|------|-------|--------------|
| 1 | Crosshair placement | 10min warmup + 2 ranked games (focus ONLY on crosshair placement) |
| 2 | Movement + deadzoning | 10min warmup + DM (Guardian only) + 2 ranked |
| 3 | Spray control | 10min warmup + Range spray practice (Vandal 10-bullet pattern) + ranked |
| 4 | Putting it together | 10min warmup + 3 ranked (track your headshot % in tracker.gg) |

If your headshot percentage increases week over week, you're improving — regardless of rank.
