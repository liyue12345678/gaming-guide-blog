Elden Ring is massive, and it doesn't hold your hand. You can wander into areas way above your level, miss crucial items permanently, and waste hours dying to something you shouldn't even be fighting yet.

These 15 tips will help you avoid the most painful mistakes.

## 1. Level Vigor First — Seriously

In most RPGs, you level your damage stat first. In Elden Ring? **Level Vigor.**

- Get Vigor to 20 before anything else
- Then aim for 30 by mid-game, 40-50 by end-game
- A dead Tarnished deals zero damage

Early-game weapons don't scale well anyway. Your damage comes from weapon upgrades, not stats. Get enough Strength/Dex to wield your weapon, then pour everything into Vigor.

## 2. Weapon Upgrades > Leveling

A +3 weapon at level 20 is stronger than a +0 weapon at level 50.

- **Smithing Stones** upgrade standard weapons (find them in mines — look for orange-tinted cave entrances on the map)
- **Somber Smithing Stones** upgrade special weapons
- Priority: unlock the Roundtable Hold blacksmith ASAP

## 3. Your First Hour — What to Actually Do

Here's the optimal start:

1. Exit the tutorial, touch the **First Step** Grace
2. Talk to White-Faced Varre, then go to **Church of Elleh**
3. Buy the **Telescope** and **Torch** from Kalé (the Santa-looking merchant)
4. Fast travel to Church of Elleh **at night** — Renna appears, gives you Spirit Calling Bell
5. Get the **Whetstone Knife** from Gatefront Ruins (cellar chest)
6. Now go to Gatefront Ruins and practice on the soldiers there

## 4. Golden Seeds and Sacred Tears — Upgrade Your Flasks

These are your most important early-game upgrades:

- **Golden Seeds**: Found beneath glowing golden saplings. Each one (or two, later) increases flask charges. You can find 3-4 in Limgrave.
- **Sacred Tears**: Found in churches. Each one makes your flasks heal more. Limgrave has 4 churches.

## 5. Don't Fight the Tree Sentinel Immediately

The gold-armored knight on horseback at the start? Come back at level 20+ with a +3 weapon. He's there to teach you that you don't have to fight everything right away.

## 6. Use Your Map — Mark and Explore

- Orange circles on the map = mines (Smithing Stones)
- Small red holes = caves/catacombs (upgrade materials, summons)
- Obelisk markers = map fragments (always grab these first)
- Place your own markers: skull = too hard, flag = interesting, diamond = cleared

## 7. Spirit Ashes Are Not Cheating

Use them. The game expects you to. **Lone Wolf Ashes** (from Renna) are excellent early-game. **Jellyfish Ashes** is tanky and applies poison. Upgrade them with Roderika at Roundtable Hold.

## 8. Roll INTO Attacks, Not Away

This is counterintuitive but crucial:
- Rolling away extends your time in the hitbox
- Rolling through an attack minimizes contact frames
- Most enemies are designed to catch back-rolling

The only exception: grab attacks and large AoE slams.

## 9. Guard Counters Are Overpowered Early Game

Block with a shield, then immediately press R2 (heavy attack). This does massive stance damage and trivializes many early enemies. Works best with a 100% physical block shield (buy the **Heater Shield** from the merchant near the Warmaster's Shack).

## 10. Don't Spread Your Stats Too Thin

Pick a direction and commit:

| Build | Primary Stat | Secondary |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| Strength | Strength | Vigor, Endurance |
| Dexterity | Dexterity | Vigor, Endurance |
| Sorcery | Intelligence | Mind, Vigor |
| Faith | Faith | Mind, Vigor |
| Bleed | Arcane | Dexterity, Vigor |

Trying to be good at everything = being good at nothing.

## 11. NPC Questlines — Don't Kill NPCs

In Elden Ring, NPCs don't respawn. If you attack one accidentally, don't kill them — you can absolve sins later. Many NPCs have long, missable questlines with unique rewards.

## 12. Learn to Parry (Eventually)

Not essential early, but parrying makes certain enemies (Crucible Knights, Bell Bearing Hunters) much easier. Buy a **Buckler** from Gatekeeper Gostoc in Stormveil Castle — it has the most forgiving parry window.

## 13. Weight Matters — Stay Under 70%

Your equip load threshold:

- **Light roll** (< 30%): fastest, most i-frames
- **Medium roll** (30-69.9%): standard, most players should stay here
- **Fat roll** (≥ 70%): terrible, don't do this

Remove armor before weapons. A fat roll will kill you faster than low defense ever will.

## 14. Remember to Level Up

Unlike other FromSoft games, you can only level up at Sites of Grace. If you're carrying a lot of Runes, go spend them. Don't be the person who loses 10,000 Runes early game.

## 15. Death Is Not Failure

You're supposed to die. The game wants you to learn from each death. Lost 5,000 Runes? That's 2 minutes of farming the Gatefront soldiers. Don't rage — learn.

## Recommended Progression Order

1. Limgrave (level 1-20) ← you are here
2. Weeping Peninsula — south, across the bridge (level 20-30)
3. Stormveil Castle (level 25-35)
4. Liurnia of the Lakes (level 35-50)
5. Caelid (level 50-70) — south is easier than north

Stay in Limgrave and Weeping Peninsula until you're comfortable. Rushing ahead will just mean more deaths.
